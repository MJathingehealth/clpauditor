# Google Stitch Prompt — Condition-Specific CLP (Back Pain)

## Primary Prompt (paste this into Stitch)

---

Design a mobile-first healthcare landing page for "Hinge Health" — a digital MSK (musculoskeletal) care company. This page is specifically for **back pain** patients who arrived from an ad. The page should feel medical yet warm, trustworthy yet modern. Think Apple Health meets One Medical.

### Brand
- Primary color: deep teal/green (#1A5632 family)
- Accent: warm coral/salmon for CTAs
- Background: clean white with light gray sections for contrast
- Typography: clean sans-serif (Inter or similar), large readable headers
- Tone: empathetic, clinical confidence without jargon

### Page structure (mobile, 375px width, scroll top to bottom):

**1. Sticky top bar**
- Small Hinge Health logo (left)
- "Get Started" button (right, coral/salmon, pill-shaped)
- Thin bar, white background, subtle bottom shadow

**2. Hero section**
- Full-width lifestyle photo of a person stretching their back, looking relieved (warm lighting, real/authentic feel)
- Overlaid text:
  - Headline: "Get lasting relief from back pain"
  - Subhead: "A personal care team — physical therapy, coaching, and a wearable sensor — covered by your employer at $0 cost."
  - Large coral CTA button: "Start My Free Assessment"
  - Small trust line below button: "Takes 5 minutes · No commitment"
- The "$0 cost" should have a small green badge/pill next to it

**3. Trust bar**
- Horizontal strip, light teal background
- 3 stats in a row:
  - "750K+ back pain members treated"
  - "75% report significant pain reduction"
  - "4.8★ App Store rating"

**4. How Hinge Health works for back pain (3 steps)**
- Section title: "How it works for your back"
- 3 vertical cards, each with an icon + title + one-liner:
  1. Icon: clipboard → "Take a 5-min assessment" / "Tell us about your back pain and goals"
  2. Icon: person with phone → "Get your care plan" / "A physical therapist builds your personalized program"
  3. Icon: body with sensor → "Start feeling better" / "Exercise therapy + a wearable sensor that guides your movement"
- Each card has a subtle teal left border or icon tint

**5. Outcome stats for back pain**
- Section title: "Back pain results from real members"
- 2 large stat cards, stacked:
  - "75% report clinically significant pain reduction within 12 weeks"
  - "68% reduction in back surgery recommendations"
- Small footnote: "Based on peer-reviewed clinical studies published in JAMA and BMC"
- Clean, high-contrast design with large bold numbers

**6. Member story**
- Section title: "Sarah's back pain story"
- A single testimonial card (not a carousel):
  - Authentic photo of a woman, casual setting
  - Quote: "I was considering surgery for my herniated disc. After 8 weeks with Hinge Health, my pain dropped from an 8 to a 2. I canceled the surgery."
  - Name: "Sarah M., Hinge Health member"
  - Condition tag: "Herniated disc · L4-L5"
- Warm, personal design — rounded card, subtle shadow

**7. Cost section**
- Section title: "Covered by your employer"
- Simple comparison:
  - "Physical therapy visits: $50-75/session"
  - "Hinge Health: $0 with your benefits"
- Green checkmark badge next to "$0"
- CTA button below: "Check My Eligibility"

**8. Final CTA block**
- Teal/dark green background, full-width
- "Ready to fix your back pain?"
- Large coral button: "Start My Free Assessment"
- Small text: "Join 750,000+ members who found relief"

**9. Simple footer**
- Hinge Health logo
- Links: Privacy · Terms · Support
- Very minimal

### Design notes
- Mobile-first (375px) — this is the primary viewport
- NO carousel components — single testimonial, not a slider
- NO accordion/FAQ section — keep it short
- Total page should feel like 5-6 thumb scrolls max
- Every section should reference "back pain" specifically — nothing generic
- High whitespace, generous padding between sections
- Accessible: 4.5:1 contrast ratios minimum, large tap targets (48px+)

## Refinement Prompts (use after first pass)

### If the hero feels generic:
> Make the hero more emotionally resonant. The photo should show a real moment of relief — someone touching their lower back with a look of surprise that the pain is gone. Add a subtle gradient overlay so the text is crisp against the image.

### If the page feels too long:
> Combine the trust bar and outcome stats into one section. Remove the "How it works" section — go straight from hero to outcomes. The page should be 4 thumb scrolls max.

### If the design feels too corporate:
> Warm it up. Use more rounded corners (16px+), add a cream/warm white background instead of pure white, and make the typography feel more like a wellness brand than a health insurance site. Think Headspace, not UnitedHealthcare.

### If the CTA doesn't stand out:
> Make the primary CTA button larger (full-width on mobile), use a deeper coral (#E85D4A), and add a subtle pulse animation or micro-shadow to draw the eye. The button should feel like the most important thing on the page.

### To generate a second condition variant (knee):
> Now create the same page but for knee pain. Replace all back pain references with knee pain. Hero headline: "Move freely again — without knee surgery." Stats: "68% reduction in knee surgery recommendations." Testimonial from a runner who returned to marathons after knee treatment. Hero image shows someone hiking or climbing stairs confidently.
